**GENERAL FAQs:**

1. Where is Al Wurood International School located in Jeddah?

New Al Wurood International School Boys’ Section is located at: Saleh bin Ibrahim Alzohari, Al Samer, Jeddah – 23466

1. What is school’s curriculum?
   New Al Wurood International School (NAWIS) offers CBSE curriculum from kindergarten to Grade XII and uses NCERT books. The curriculum at NAWIS is based upon strong academic roots. It emphasizes and relies upon what is best in traditional independent school education: scholarly excellence supported by a caring, pastoral and tutorial system, and a wide - ranging co- curricular programme. We are continually adapting and reacting to the changing demands of modern education: new subjects are added, new teaching techniques adopted, and there is an increasing awareness of the need to provide programmes of study that match individual needs and skills.
2. Is multi language instruction offered?

The medium of instruction is English. Hindi and Arabic are offered as Second Language, and Arabic, Malayalam and Urdu are offered as Third Language.

1. What extracurricular activities are provided for the students?

The school provides opportunity in a multitude of sports and recreational programmes. Training is provided in Football, Volleyball, Badminton, Athletics, etc. Physical training (PT) is carried out regularly on a weekly basis. Besides these, students will have a wide choice of activities according to their interests, such as Painting, Visual Performing Arts, Public speaking, Debate, Scouts and Guides and Art classes. The Students from I to XII are divided into four houses namely Sapphire, Topaz, Amber and Ruby. They represent these houses in the New Al Wurood Annual Sports Day, where their talent has always been recognized, encouraged and nurtured.

1. Are there parents and teachers’ meetings?

Parent-Teacher meetings are conducted regularly to discuss the academic performance of their wards. Constructive suggestions from parents are always welcome and they will be given due consideration. Suggestions and matters concerning the student’s studies and discipline may be send to the principal in writing on which further discussions can be arranged, if found necessary.

1. Is the school approved by Ministry of Education?
2. Yes, New Al Wurood International School (NAWIS) is approved by the Ministry of Education of Saudi Arabia.
3. What kind of transportation is provided for students?

The school provides transport facility to the students through its own buses for transporting the students for different localities to the school and vice-versa. The overall transport operations are insured and systematically monitored daily.

1. Do you have Scouts and Guides programme in your school?

Scouts and Guides provide the impetus for students to be ever ready to face challenges in this competitive environment. The scouts and guide movement also has regular camps and Outbound Programme which facilitate team and collaborative learning and prepares them for leading and organizing programmes. There have been camporees, jamborees and camps organized under the able guidance of Bharat Scouts and Guides, India in KSA, India and Japan. These camps enable a child to be prepared for life eventually. Cubs and Bulbuls (Grade 2 & 3) Cubs and Bulbuls programme is the progressive advancement in scouting designed to satisfy the needs of the students of age group between 5 – 10 years. The purpose of the programme is to develop the inherent potential of the children by providing them several structured activities to make them physically, mentally, socially, spiritually and emotionally strong and useful citizens and members of local, national and international communities. School level camps of the Cubs and Bulbuls will be conducted to achieve this goal in which opportunities are provided to the Cubs and bulbuls to enjoy the specially carved out activities for them. Bunnies (KG) It is the kiddie’s version of Scouts and Guides Programme. The Bunnies boys and girls would become Cubs and Bulbuls between 7 – 10 years of age and later Scouts and Guides respectively. The programme will help the children involve in progressive discovery, acceptance of responsibility, and training towards the development of character, acquisition of competence, self- reliance, dependability, and capacities to cooperate and to lead. This will be achieved through the activities included during the sessions. This will be included as a part of the regular school curriculum to impart the life skills to the child.

1. How do I arrange a meeting with my kid’s class teacher?

The contact number of the class teacher will be shared with the parents. Parents can interact with the class teacher through the mobile app. Face to face meeting can be arranged with prior appointment.

1. Does the school provide in-service training for the teachers?

All round development of Teachers is an ongoing process at New Al Wurood Intl School. The Teachers manage the classrooms effectively bringing an invigorating atmosphere to their classes. To improve their quality of teaching, teachers attend regular workshops and in service programmes. They are at liberty to avail information from a well-equipped library, audio visual aids and other labs. They have access to internet and web browsing to help them know more about latest trends and developments in their respective fields. Regular fieldtrips are organized for the teachers to make teaching more effective, enjoyable and collaborative.

1. Does Al Wurood have an Arabic and Qur’an learning program?

We have become the first international school in the Kingdom to start Understand Quran: the Easy Way. It is the easiest and the most effective learning method developed by Understand Quran Academy, Hyderabad. The lessons and Arabic grammar are taught using the most modern methodology. Total Physical Interaction (TPI) and Neuro Linguistic Programming (NLP) are also employed in the programme. The message of the Quran along with its Arabic grammar is taught in an innovative manner by designing power point slides and easy to use textbooks as, workbooks, posters, games and vocabulary cards. The classes are conducted up to grade VIII. Arabic Language and Islamic studies as per the curriculum prescribed by the MoE is also adopted in the school in all classes.

1. Is Al Wurood affiliated with any other schools?

Al Wurood International School is run by BEST Schools (Bridgeway Educational Systems & Training). We have educational institutions in Middle East countries including Al Khozama International School Dammam - www.alkhozama.org
